Frequently Asked Questions

All REAL questions that we have been asked frequently.

Why Baltimore?

  • Ashley's Aunt and Uncle live on a beautiful property just outside Baltimore and were gracious enough to let us have the ceremony and reception there. Plus, the location lends itself to some honeymoon ideas...

Are children allowed at your wedding?

  • Yes, bring the whole family!

Is there a dress code?

  • Typical wedding attire. Remember that it will all be outdoors, so dress accordingly.
  • Using this site as a guide, Dressy Casual sounds about right. In any case, no cut-off jean shorts.

Do you have a wedding registry?

  • Ryan's parents showed us an excerpt from the book Offbeat Bride by Ariel Stallings that that describes our feelings better than we can:

  • "Traditional registries make the most sense for couples building a house together... After six years together, we did not need a goat. Nor did we need any sheets or plates or candlesticks. We had collected our own mishmash of secondhand flatware and had purchased several sets of our own sheets. We didn't need any crystal or china. A large number of the wedding websites I browsed seemed to focus very heavily on the registry and showers, and less on the actual significance of the event. For me, that's a turnoff - the idea of having my seventy-year-old grandmother there is far more inmportant than whether or not she sent me a gift, especially when we live in a world where so many people don't have anything and many others, even in this country, have very little."

  • We are not trying to be difficult, but with all that in mind, it is hard for us to ask for things. We know that most of our guests will be traveling for the wedding, and that has a cost. What we really want is for all of our family and friends to be in the same place celebrating with us. If you insist on giving us gifts, we do have some big events that will be coming up in the future, so please keep that in mind.

Will Charlie (the dog) be at the wedding?

  • Yes, who else would bring the ring up to the altar?
